Canada's Soccer History

Football or Soccer as it’s better known in North America has been around in Canada since the foundation of the Canadian Soccer Association in 1912. Although the sport has existed here for a century Canada as a nation has struggled to become a global superpower in the sport. The men’s national team's first experience on the global stage happened at the FIFA World Cup in 1986 hosted by neighboring Mexico. Unfortunately they couldn’t manage to get out of the group stage or score a goal in Canada’s inaugural tournament. Through the long road for qualification a nation was united in a new sporting interest, although it would be a long road to see them in a World Cup again.

Qualifying was a massive achievement for newly soccer-crazed Canada. It showcased the men’s team on the global stage, and garnered enthusiasm and interest across the country. After the success of qualification in 1986 Canada unfortunately disappeared from the spotlight of the World Cup. Canada was unsuccessful in qualification for over 30 years. The Canadian national team had some exciting moments regionally over those 30 years, notably winning the Gold Cup in 2000, a competition of CONCACAF based nations.

Next came the moment a nation had waited 36 long years for, Canada had qualified for the World Cup in Qatar. This time was different. Canada now had a fresh crop of young, exciting talent with many plying their trade in the elite leagues of Europe with star Bayern Munich player Alphonso Davies leading the way. Notably they scored their first ever World Cup goal with an Alphonso Davies strike against European giants Croatia. They could not manage to break out of the group stage which also featured difficult tests against Belgium and Morocco. It was not all doom and gloom though, Canadian soccer was now on the ascendancy.

Now moving forward to 2026, Canada co-hosts the World Cup alongside the USA and Mexico. The first game kicks off at BMO Field in Toronto on June 12th against Bosnia. With the home crowd behind them, and a young team brimming with talent the target is different this time, moving on from the group stage is the goal. Even more exciting than the prospect of progressing in the World Cup is that a new generation will be inspired by this World Cup, hopefully transitioning this new excitement into regularly being a contender on the World Stage.
