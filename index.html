Canada's Soccer History

Soccer's been around in Canada for ages, but it really struggled to become a big deal nationally for a long time. The men's team finally made it to their first FIFA World Cup in 1986 down in Mexico. They got knocked out in the group stage without scoring a single goal, but just qualifying sparked way more interest in the sport across the country.

Qualifying felt huge for the soccer crazed in Canada. It really put the men's team on the map and got way more people across the country excited about soccer. After 1986 though, Canada basically disappeared from the World Cup scene. They couldn't qualify again for more than 30 years. The team had some decent moments regionally here and there, but nothing that got them back into the global spotlight.

Then came the moment the nation waited, 36 years for, now they're in Qatar. This time, with a fresh crop of talent like Alphonso Davies leading the way, things felt different. They actually scored their first-ever World Cup goal (an Alphonso Davies goal against European giants Croatia), even though they still didn't make it out of the group. It was a clear sign that Canadian soccer was finally on the rise.

Now, fast forward to 2026, and Canada gets to co-host the World Cup alongside the US and Mexico. Playing at home with the crowd behind them, plus all this growing young talent, the aim is definitely to get past the group stage for the first time and really compete. Even bigger than that, is the hope that a strong showing will inspire the next wave of kids and help turn Canada into a regular contender on the world stage.
